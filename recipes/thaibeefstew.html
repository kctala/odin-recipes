Slow Cooker Thai Beef Stew

Description

This slightly spicy beef stew recipe was pulled from a book called Meals Made Simple, and was given to Kate by her mom Valerie Ainsworth.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 3 pounds beef stew meat, trimmed of fat
  • 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp peeled and minced fresh ginger
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup Thai red curry paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tsp sea salt
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cups julienned carrots
  • 1 cup peeled and julienned jicama
  • fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tbsp of coconut oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Working in batches, brown the meat on all sides.
  2. Use a slotted sppon to transfer each batch of browned meat directly to the slow cooker, then continue browning. Wipe out the skillet between batches if a lot of liquid has accumulated at the bottom to ensure even browning.
  3. Wipe out the skillet and add the remaining 1 tbsp of coconut oil. Saute the onion, garlic, and ginger over medium-high heat for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and stur continuously to release the browned bits on the bottom of the pan.
  5. Add the tomato paste, curry paste, fish sauce, lime juice, and salt, then pour the mixture over the beef in the slow cooker.
  6. Valerie addition: add 1 cup of water
  7. Cook on high for 5 hours or low for 8 hours. Add the broccoli, carrots, and jicama during the last 30 minutes if cooking on high, or the last hour if cooking on low. Serve garnished with cilantro.
